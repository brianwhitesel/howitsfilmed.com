## The seven mistakes (slides 2-8)

Each slide is the same template. Big number, mistake title, two sentences of explanation, one underlined fix. Same layout across all seven so the swipe feels like a sequence.

**Slide 2 — No shot list, just vibes**
I walked onto set with the scene in my head and three loose ideas. By hour two I was making up shots, the talent was bored, and we missed the close-up that mattered.
Fix: write the shot list before call time. Even three lines per setup.

**Slide 3 — Underbudgeting time, not money**
I planned the budget down to the dollar and the schedule down to "around 2pm." Money came in on target. Schedule blew up on every shoot.
Fix: schedule every setup, every move, every meal. Time is the thing that breaks first.

**Slide 4 — No call sheet, no contact list**
The DP was forty minutes late because I texted him the wrong address. The talent showed up in the wrong wardrobe because nobody told her. There was no document. Just my memory.
Fix: one call sheet. One source of truth. Sent the night before.

**Slide 5 — Forgetting the gear nobody talks about**
Cards, batteries, a roll of black gaff, a hard drive, a stinger. I would remember the camera and forget the things that make the camera work.
Fix: a gear checklist that lives in a place you actually look. Not a note in your phone you wrote at 1am.

**Slide 6 — Skipping the location scout**
"We'll figure it out on the day." On the day, the room had three buzzing fluorescent tubes, one outlet, and a window that pointed straight at the talent's eyes at 4pm.
Fix: scout in person. Note power, sound, light direction, parking. Photograph all four corners.

**Slide 7 — No release forms**
The shoot went great. Two months later the client asked for a usage rights doc and I had nothing. The talent was nice about it. They are not always nice about it.
Fix: have the release ready before they show up. Sign on set. Filed before they leave.

**Slide 8 — Building from scratch every time**
Every shoot I rebuilt the same documents from a blank page. Different format every time. No version I could trust. Hours wasted, mistakes copied.
Fix: build the templates once. Reuse forever. The system is the moat.
